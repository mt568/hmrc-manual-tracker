### Disposals of Stock - Decision Flow

### Are you using the cash basis?

* Yes - see guidance at either:
  + ([BIM70025](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim70025)) for accounting periods ending on or before 5 April 2024
  + ([BIM72025](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim72025)) for accounting periods ending on or after 6 April 2024
* No - continue below

### Is the stock sold or transferred for valuable consideration to a person who will carry on a trade in the UK and deduct the cost of the stock in computing their trading profits?

* No - the value for the stock is the amount which would have been realised if sold in the open market ([BIM33525](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33525))
* Yes - continue below

### Is the stock subject to specific rules (professional work in progress or herd basis animals)?

* Yes - apply specific legislation ([BIM33510](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33510))
* No - continue below

### Are the vendor and purchaser connected?

The definition for connected persons is here: [valuation on discontinuance of business: meanings](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33495)

* No - S176 ITTOIA 2005 or S165 CTA 2009 apply. The amount realised is the closing value for the vendor and the opening value for the purchaser ([BIM33480](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33480))
* Yes - continue below

### Is the consideration received the arm’s length price?

* Yes - arm's length price is used as closing value for vendor and opening value for purchaser ([BIM33495](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33495))
* No - continue below

### Is the consideration received greater than the arm’s length price?

* Yes - arm’s length price is used as closing value for vendor and opening value for purchaser ([BIM33495](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33495))
* No - continue below

### Is the arm’s length price more than both the acquisition value and the price received?

Parties may elect to substitute the greater of value received and acquisition value ([BIM33495](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33495)). Otherwise use the arm’s length value.
