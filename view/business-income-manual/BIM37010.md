S34 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005, S54 Corporation Tax Act 2009

## Introduction and layout of guidance

To understand the subject of ‘wholly and exclusively’ you need to place it in the statutory context of what is involved in computing profits for tax purposes. The relevant legislation is described in the following sections of guidance:
- [BIM37020 — Statutory background: legislative starting point](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37020)
- [BIM37025 — Statutory background: expenses rule for employees](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37025)
- [BIM37030 — Statutory background: tax is charged on the full amount of profits](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37030)
- [BIM37035 — Statutory background: the statutory prohibition](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37035)
- [BIM37045 — Statutory background: appeal to be on a point of law](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37045)
