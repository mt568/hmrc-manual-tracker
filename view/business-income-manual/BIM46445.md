## Need to establish the facts

You need to examine the precise nature of the claims by the parties to the proceedings to consider the extent to which the fees are to be disallowed. The fees may be disallowed because they:

* are capital expenditure
* fail the ‘wholly and exclusively’ test (see [BIM42100](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42100) onwards), or
* are a loss which is not connected with or arises from a trade

The outcome of the proceedings (i.e. whether the taxpayer ‘wins’ or ‘loses’) does not determine if the expense is deductible. See both [BIM40100](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim40100) onwards and [BIM42950](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42950) onwards for compensation and damages and [BIM42515](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42515) for fines.
