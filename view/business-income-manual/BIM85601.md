## S66, S67 Income Tax Act 2007

Farmers do not control the two main factors affecting the results of their businesses; market prices and the weather. These and other factors (such as outbreaks of foot and mouth disease) may cause even the most efficiently managed farms to show losses from time to time. Various forms of loss relief are available to help in such cases (see [BIM85000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85000) onwards). In a minority of cases, claims for tax relief in respect of farming losses can present particular difficulty because:

* all UK farming is treated as the carrying on of a trade and is thus eligible for the loss relief provisions applying to trades - whether or not it meets the normal commercial criteria of trading (see [BIM55110](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55110));
* people with income from other sources sometimes take up farming for the sake of recreation or the lifestyle or status which it offers rather than for genuinely commercial reasons.

Because of this, Parliament has enacted rules which prevent some farming losses being offset against non-farming income.

## Restrictions on trade loss relief against general income

Where losses are sustained in farming activities of an essentially uncommercial nature, trade loss relief against general income (see [BIM85015](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85015)) is not available where either:

1. where tax adjusted losses before capital allowances were incurred in each of the five previous tax years (see [BIM85605](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85605) and [BIM85620](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85620) onwards) (the ‘five year rule’), or
2. the trade was not run on a commercial basis and with a view to the realisation of commercial profits (see [BIM85605](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85605) and [BIM85701](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85701)).

The five year rule is generally more straightforward to use as it involves a mechanical test. It should be applied, subject to [BIM85640](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85640) and [BIM85645](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85645), in all cases where the conditions (see [BIM85620](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85620) onwards) are satisfied. Cases where (1) above does not apply, but where the activities appear clearly uncommercial, should be considered for challenge under (2) above, subject to [BIM85615](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85615) and [BIM85701](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85701) onwards.

## Restriction on early trade loss relief

Where relief by way of carry back in respect of losses sustained in the commencing years of a trade is claimed, a stricter test of commerciality than that at (2) above applies (see [BIM85045](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85045)).

## Companies

The guidance in this chapter applies to unincorporated taxpayers. Similar restrictions apply to companies within the charge to Corporation Tax and these are described at [CTM04600](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm04600) onwards and [CTM04710](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm04710). Broadly, the guidance at [BIM85620](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85620)-[BIM85645](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85645) can be applied to companies, substituting ‘accounting period’ for ‘tax year’.

## Cash Basis

Farmers calculating their profits using the cash basis must use the loss relief rules at [BIM70005](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim70005).
