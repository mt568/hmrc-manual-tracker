title: 'Athletes: Timing of Income'
---

The time at which an athlete's taxable income should be taxed depends on whether the individual is considered to be carrying on a trade or a hobby, see [BIM50605](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50605). For details of the types of income which the athlete might receive, see [BIM50610](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50610).

If the athlete is trading, deciding when to include the income as a trading receipt will involve consideration of the relevant agreements and all the surrounding circumstances in order to apply the correct accounting treatment (see [BIM31080](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim31080)).

If the athlete is not trading and the income is taxable under S687 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005 (see [BIM50606](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50606)), the amount taxed is the amount arising in the tax year: see s688 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005.
