title: 'Authors and literary profits: royalties to person other than author'
---

Copyright royalties received by an individual other than the author, composer etc (unless they form part of the receipts of a trade or represent post-cessation receipts - see [BIM50740](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50740)), are chargeable to Income Tax as miscellaneous income. A deduction should be given, where appropriate, for agents' commission.

Copyright royalties received by a company are dealt with under the intangible assets regime. See [CIRD10000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird10000) onwards.

Where it is claimed that royalties are not chargeable because of the decision in Hume v Asquith [1968] 45 TC 251, the case should be referred to Business Profits (Technical).
