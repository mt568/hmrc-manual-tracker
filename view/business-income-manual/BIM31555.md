title: 'Value Added Tax: business entertainment'
---

The VAT borne on business entertainment expenditure is generally not to be deducted as input tax. For VAT, ‘business entertainment’ means, subject to certain exceptions, entertainment (including hospitality of any kind) provided by a taxable person in connection with a business carried on by him. You should ensure that the cost of any business entertainment disallowed in calculating taxable trade profits (see [BIM45000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim45000) onwards) includes the amount of the VAT related to it.
