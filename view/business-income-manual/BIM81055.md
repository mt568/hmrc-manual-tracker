  accounting date'

S219 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005

Where there was a change of accounting date in the previous tax year, but the three conditions in [BIM81045](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim81045) were not met for the previous year, a change of accounting date is treated as taking place in the next year if accounts are prepared to the new accounting date in that year.

The three conditions in [BIM81045](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim81045) must be satisfied for the next year if the basis period for that year is to end with the new accounting date.

Examples 5 and 6 at [BIM81060](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim81060) illustrate this.
