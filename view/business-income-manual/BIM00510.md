## What the BIM is for

This manual is to help you compute trading income for tax purposes or check calculations. It supplements the basic guidance provided in the self-assessment tax return and help sheets.

It is written primarily for HMRC staff but it can also assist customers and their professional advisers. You are seeing the whole manual with nothing hidden.

You can get basic information on how to compute trading profits from the self-employed notes pages of the tax return and the related help sheets. The helpsheet for customers with annual business turnover under the VAT threshold who need to complete the short version of the self-employed supplementary pages of the return (SA103S) can be found on GOV.UK at <https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/self-assessment-self-employment-short-sa103s>. The helpsheet for those required to complete the full version (SA103F) can be found at <https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/self-assessment-self-employment-full-sa103f>.

Agents can also find guidance in the Business Profits Toolkit at <https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/hmrc-business-profits-toolkit>.

## How to use the BIM

The main way to get to the BIM pages you need is drilling down through the contents pages.

We strongly recommend that you study the contents pages of each chapter of the manual to make sure that the further detail is not taken out of context. If you aren't on a contents page, you can find contents by selecting the 'Business Income Manual' link in the breadcrumb trail at the top of each page.
