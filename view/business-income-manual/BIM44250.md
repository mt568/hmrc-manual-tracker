Deductions for providing shares to employees fall into the following categories:

* Providing shares through Share Incentive Plans (SIPs), see [BIM44251](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44251).
* Providing shares (other than through a SIP) which meet the qualifying conditions for a statutory Corporation Tax deduction (referred to in this guidance as ‘qualifying shares’), see [BIM44260](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44260).
* Providing shares which are not qualifying shares (referred to in this guidance as ‘non-qualifying shares’), see [BIM44450](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44450).

## Qualifying shares

‘Qualifying shares’ for this purpose are those which meet the qualifying conditions for a statutory CT deduction. These are shares which give the employees a real stake in the company or group by which they are employed. Full details of the qualifying requirements are at [BIM44285](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44285).

In practice it is reasonable to assume that shares provided under Tax advantaged schemes (see [BIM44010](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44010)) will be qualifying shares as the qualifying conditions are very similar.

Shares provided under other employee share schemes (see [BIM44005](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44005)) may also be qualifying shares.

## Non-qualifying shares

The following are not qualifying shares:

* Partly-paid shares.
* Redeemable shares.
* Preference shares.
* Unlisted shares in a subsidiary company (unless its parent company is a listed and non-close company).
* Shares in a company which is not sufficiently linked to the employing company at the date the employee acquires the shares, or at any earlier date on which the employee was given a right (such as a share option) to acquire the shares.
