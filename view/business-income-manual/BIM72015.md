title: 'Cash basis: receipts: overview'
---

Cash basis receipts are any amounts received in connection
with the trade during the the
tax year
and include:

* Cash
  and cheques
* Tips,
  fees and commissions
* Value
  of any payments ‘in kind’ for work done or goods sold
* Some
  capital receipts (see BIM72020)
* The
  value of trading stock on cessation of trade (see BIM72025)
* The
  value of work in progress on cessation of profession or vocation
  (see BIM72025)
* Just
  and reasonable additions in respect of non-commercial (non-arm’s length)
  transactions (see below)
* Certain
  transitional adjustments on entering the cash basis (see BIM72060 onwards).

Any cash basis receipts which are received after the
cessation of a trade using the cash basis are treated as post-cessation
receipts (see [BIM90030](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim90030)).

**Amounts not reflecting commercial transactions**

For businesses using the cash basis, there is no requirement
to bring into account as a receipt the value of any goods taken from the
business for personal use (see [BIM33630](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33630)).
Instead, a just and reasonable adjustment should be made to reflect the
uncommercial transaction. So, for example, the cost of any such goods might be
disallowed as an expense.
