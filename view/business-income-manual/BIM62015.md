  of trade'

S57 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income)Act 2005, S61 Corporation Tax Act 2009

A trade does not commence until the trader is in a position to provide the relevant goods or services and offers to do so, i.e. has an open door for business (see [BIM80500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim80500) onwards). Accordingly, the rules as to pre-trading expenses apply to any expenditure before that time.

The date on which a colliery or a mine commences its trade is normally the date on which minerals are first produced for sale, but is a question of fact to be determined in each particular case.

See [OT20250](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/oil-taxation-manual/ot20250) concerning the date of commencement of operations on the Continental Shelf.
