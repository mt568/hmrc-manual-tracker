The company’s purpose is established by reference to the purpose of those who are entitled to act for it. See [BIM38210](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim38210).

## Companies under common control

For companies under common control it may be relevant to consider if the purpose of an expense was wholly and exclusively for the trade carried on by the paying company. If two companies which share the same directors benefit from an expense incurred by one of them, facts independent of the say-so of the directors will be needed to establish that the purpose was solely for the trade of the company incurring the expense. See:

| Case | Guidance |
| --- | --- |
| Marshall Richards Machine Co Ltd v Jewitt [1956] 36TC511 | BIM37790 |
| Vodafone Cellular Ltd & Others v Shaw [1997] 69TC376 | [BIM38220](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim38220) |
| Garforth v Tankard Carpets Ltd [1980] 53TC342 | [BIM38210](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim38210) |

## Group service companies

Where a group service company incurs expenses on behalf of another group company or companies it may be carrying on a trade of providing a service to those companies, subject to the guidance at [BIM20000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim20000) onwards.

Where the group service company is carrying on a trade and where the company on whose behalf the services are performed is charged other than an arm’s length amount, the transfer pricing rules may apply - see [INTM410000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/international-manual/intm410000) onwards.

If the group service company is not carrying on a trade of which the provision of the services in question is part (and, in this context, see [BIM80535](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim80535) regarding a company carrying on more than one trade) then:

* if it is carrying on a different trade the expenses are disallowable as a deduction in computing the profits of the group service company as they are not incurred wholly and exclusively for the purposes of its trade, or
* if it is not carrying on any trade the ‘wholly and exclusively’ test has no application, in which case it might also be necessary to consider whether it is a company with investment business - see [CTM08000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm08000) onwards and particularly [CTM08180](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm08180).

The following flowchart illustrates the process which should be followed in considering whether expenditure should be disallowed on ‘wholly and exclusively’ grounds (or not allowed as management expenses under S1219 Corporation Tax Act 2009 - see [CTM08180](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm08180)) in situations where the transfer pricing rules would otherwise apply.

[The flowchart (Word 110KB)](http://www.hmrc.gov.uk/gds/bim/attachments/bim42140_flowchart.doc) applies when costs are incurred (or should be incurred) in the provision or receipt of intra-group services.
