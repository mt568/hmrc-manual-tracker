title: 'Transactions in land: Conditions'
---

S756, S757 Income Tax Act 2007, S819, S820 Corporation Tax Act 2010

In order for the transactions in land provisions to apply a number of conditions have to be satisfied.

Firstly, either:

* the land (see [BIM60450](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60450)) is acquired with the sole or main aim of realising a gain from disposing all or part of the land,
* any property deriving its value from the land (see [BIM60455](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60455)) is acquired with the sole or main aim of realising a gain from disposing of all or part of the land,
* the land is held as trading stock (see [BIM33000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33000)), or
* the land is developed (see [BIM60460](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60460)) with the sole or main object of realising a gain from disposing of all or part of the land when developed.

For guidance on investigating the motive of the taxpayer, see [BIM60320](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60320).

Secondly, assuming that any one of the above is the case, for the rules to apply:

* a gain of a capital nature must be obtained on the disposal of all or part of the land (see [BIM60470](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60470)),
* all or part of the land must be situated in the UK, and
* the person obtaining the gain either on their own or another person’s behalf must fall into one of the categories listed in [BIM60325](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60325).

For details of what constitutes a ‘disposal’ for the purposes of these rules, see [BIM60465](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60465).

For a table outlining the territorial scope of the provisions, see [BIM60315](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60315).
