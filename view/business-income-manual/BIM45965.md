S59 Corporation Tax Act 2009

## Use of patent

### Corporation Tax

It is a specific statutory rule that no deduction is allowed for royalties or other sums paid for the use of patents. For most patent royalties, however, this rule is overridden by the rules of the intangible fixed assets regime (see [CIRD10000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird10000) onwards).

### Income Tax

There is no statutory rule prohibiting a deduction for patent royalties in calculating trade profits. A deduction is admissible on ordinary principles.

## Copyright royalties

For Income Tax, copyright royalties paid for the purposes of a trade are admissible as a deduction. For Corporation Tax purposes, copyright royalties are dealt with under the rules of the intangible fixed assets regime (see [CIRD10000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird10000) onwards).

## Registered designs & trade marks

A design registered under the Registered Designs Act 1949 and the Copyright, Designs and Patents Act 1988 Part III, is, by those Acts, made the subject of a design right, which is similar to a copyright. Royalties are dealt with for tax purposes in the same way as copyright.
