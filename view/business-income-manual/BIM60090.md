title: 'Measuring the profits (particular trades): land: trading transactions: land
  acquired by inheritance or gift'
---

Where land is acquired by inheritance or gift, its subsequent disposal will not ordinarily be chargeable as trading income: **Hudson's Bay Company v Stevens** [1909] 5TC424 and **Williams v Davies** [1945] 26TC371.

A possible exception to this general rule is where, following acquisition, the recipient decides to use the property to start trading in land. This would be the commencement of a supervening trade (see [BIM60060](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60060)).
