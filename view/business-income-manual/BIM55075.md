title: 'Farming in tax law: tax treatment of share farming agreements'
---

S996(1) Income Tax Act 2007, S1125 Corporation Tax Act 2010

Both parties to a genuine share farming agreement may be accepted as farming (see [BIM55051](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55051)) as they have concurrent rights to the land and both are contributing to an undertaking of husbandry thereon (but see [BIM55080](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55080) - [BIM55085](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55085)).

For guidance on the application of the herd basis in share farming cases, see [BIM55640](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55640).
