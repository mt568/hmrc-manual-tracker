## Two main questions

The deduction of compensation or damages payments depends, broadly, on whether they were incurred:

* wholly and exclusively for trade purposes (see [BIM37000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37000) onwards for general guidance and [BIM42955](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42955) on compensation/damages payments), and
* on revenue account (see [BIM35000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim35000) onwards for the capital/revenue distinction and [BIM42960](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42960) on compensation/damages payments)

### Compensation for loss of office paid on retirement

If compensation for loss of office is paid on retirement, the guidance in [EIM15042](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-income-manual/eim15042) should be considered. Guidance on compensation or damages payments in substantial cases may be obtained from CTISA (Technical).

There may also be other costs associated with making the compensation payment:

* for legal costs, see [BIM46400](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim46400) onwards
* for contributions to approved pension schemes, see [BIM46000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim46000) onwards
* for redundancy payments, see [BIM47200](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim47200) onwards
