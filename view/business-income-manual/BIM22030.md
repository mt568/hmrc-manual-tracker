title: 'Meaning of trade: exceptions and alternatives: activities not connected with
  an existing trade'
---

A trader’s operations that cannot be regarded either, as operations of his ordinary business or as ancillary to it, should be considered independently in the light of the principles indicated in [BIM20050](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim20050) onwards.

If they amount to the carrying on of a trade (or produce liability as miscellaneous income) and they have some connections with the operations of the trader’s ordinary business, they may in practice be merged with it.
