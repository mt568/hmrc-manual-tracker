title: 'Specific receipts: voluntary receipts: introduction'
---

A voluntary receipt is one which the payer is under no legal obligation to make. In determining the correct treatment of a voluntary payment received you need to consider:

* whether the sum is a receipt of the trade, and
* if so, whether it is a receipt on revenue or on capital account.

This chapter covers the first bullet only. As regards the second see [BIM40100](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim40100) onwards (compensation) and [BIM40450](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim40450) onwards (grants and subsidies).

As always, it will be necessary to collect and consider all the facts and circumstances surrounding the payment. Where the payer may be seeking a deduction you should liaise with the recipient’s office to check and compare the information obtained. Where the receipt is not a trading receipt to be included in computing taxable trade profits, the possibility of liability to Capital Gains Tax should be considered; see [CG12940](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/capital-gains-manual/cg12940) onwards.
