title: 'Averaging: profits for the purpose of averaging'
---

S221 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005

Profits for the purpose of averaging are the profits of the qualifying trade, profession or vocation which are chargeable to Income Tax as trading income for the tax year. The profits to be taken are:

* after the deduction or addition of capital allowances and balancing charges
* after any adjustment under the legislation for compensation for the compulsory slaughter of animals (see [BIM55180](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55180))
* before any deduction for losses.

Losses count as Nil.

When profits have been averaged, the new averaged profits are used in any claim to averaging in later years (see [BIM84140](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84140)).
