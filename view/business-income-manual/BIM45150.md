This chapter covers various specific types of charitable gifts and donations including those to educational establishments and gifts of medical supplies. Any doubt or difficulty in the application of this guidance and, in particular, if there is evidence that the donor, or anyone connected with the donor, received a benefit as a result of the gift, should be referred to Business Profits.

Associated guidance can be found as follows:

* voluntary organisations & charities (see [BIM24792](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim24792) onwards)
* benevolent gifts by traders to charities (see [BIM45072](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim45072))
* subscriptions (see [BIM47400](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim47400) onwards)
* charity special appeals (see [BIM47410](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim47410))
* Gift Aid, which can be claimed both by sole traders and partnerships (see the HMRC Charities Internet site at <http://www.hmrc.gov.uk/businesses/giving/self-employed.htm>)
* Qualifying Donations Relief (which can be claimed by companies, see HMRC Charities Internet site at <http://www.hmrc.gov.uk/businesses/giving/companies.htm>)
