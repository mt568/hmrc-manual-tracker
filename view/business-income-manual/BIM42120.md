title: 'Deductions - scope of: tax avoidance'
---

Where, as part of a scheme for tax avoidance, a sum in excess of the commercial price is paid, there is a presumption that the excess was not expended for the purposes of the trade and that excess is not therefore allowable as a deduction. See the judgment of Lord Reid on page 82F and that of Lord Wilberforce on page 93 **of Ransom v Higgs** and **Kilmorie (Aldridge) Ltd v Dickinson** [1974] 50TC1 (see [BIM38400](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim38400)).
