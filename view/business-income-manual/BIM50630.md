title: 'Athletes: Miscellaneous income'
---

Chapter 8 of Part 5 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005 taxes income not otherwise charged, also known as miscellaneous income. These charging provisions may sometimes be considered as an alternative where the athlete’s activities fall short of trading. However, it can only apply to income contractually received in return for work done, services rendered or facilities provided (see [BIM50606](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50606), Example 2). It cannot apply to voluntary grants given for nothing in return; see Scott v Ricketts [1967] 44 TC 303, which is discussed in [BIM65151](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim65151).

In terms of when these payments are recognised as income for tax purposes, see [BIM50625](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50625).
