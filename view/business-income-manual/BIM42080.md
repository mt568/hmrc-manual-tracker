S31 Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005, S51 Corporation Tax Act 2009

In general, rules which permit a deduction take priority over rules which prohibit a deduction.

The following prohibitive rules, however, take priority over permissive rules.

| Income Tax (Trading and Other Income) Act 2005 section | Corporation Tax Act 2009 section | Prohibitive rule | Further guidance |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 36 | 1288 | Unpaid remuneration | [BIM47130](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim47130) onwards |
| 38 | 1290 | Employee benefit contributions | [BIM44500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44500) |
| 48 | 56 | Car hire | [BIM47780](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim47780) onwards |
| 55 | 1304 | Crime-related payments | [BIM43100](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43100) onwards |

‘Prohibitive rule’ extends, for this purpose, beyond the list of provisions at [BIM42060](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42060) to include any provision relating to the calculation of trade profits which might be read as prohibiting or restricting the amount of a deduction.
