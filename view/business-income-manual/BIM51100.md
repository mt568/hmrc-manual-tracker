## Introduction and layout of guidance

Barristers and advocates are able to use an alternative basis of calculating their taxable profits in the seven years following first holding themselves out for work (see [BIM51120](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim51120)), provided they had elected to use the alternative basis for the tax year 2012/13.

This chapter contains the following:
- [BIM51110 — Barristers: general provisions](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim51110)
- [BIM51120 — Barristers: barristers and advocates in early years of practice](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim51120)
- [BIM51130 — Barristers: expenditure on clothing](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim51130)
