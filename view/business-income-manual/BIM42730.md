title: 'Specific deductions: bad & doubtful debts: recovered after allowance'
---

The following guidance does not apply to bad money debts of companies which are dealt with under the loan relationship rules. See [BIM42701](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42701).

Where a deduction for a bad or doubtful debt has been made and the taxpayer subsequently recovers the debt or an amount in excess of its written-down value, the amount recovered or the excess should be brought into credit in the year of recovery (see **Bristow v William Dickinson & Co Ltd** (1946) 27 TC 157).

As regards the treatment of debts recovered after the discontinuance of a business, see [BIM42745](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42745).
