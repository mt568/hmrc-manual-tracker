title: 'Authors and literary profits: introduction'
---

The profits made by individuals resident in the United Kingdom from literary, composing etc activities which they carry on (including profits from the literary, dramatic, film, musical etc copyright in their works) are chargeable to Income Tax as:

* profits from a profession of an author, composer etc; or
* miscellaneous income, if the activities do not amount to the exercise of a profession.

As regards:

* copyright royalties received by a person other than the author, composer etc, see [BIM50725](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50725)
* copyright royalties etc within the scope of S906 Income Tax Act 2007, received by persons abroad, see [SAIM9130](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/savings-and-investment-manual/saim9130)
* casual income arising under contracts made in the United Kingdom or abroad, see [BIM100110](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim100110).

References to copyright in [BIM50701](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50701) - [BIM50745](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50745) should be treated as including public lending right ([BIM50755](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim50755)).
