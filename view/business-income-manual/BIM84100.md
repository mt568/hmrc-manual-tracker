title: 'Averaging: checklist'
---

To carry out an initial review of an averaging claim, check that:

1. The trade, profession or vocation is a qualifying one (see [BIM84050](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84050)).
2. Where the trade is farming, market gardening or intensive rearing of livestock or fish, it is carried on in the UK (see [BIM84050](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84050)).
3. The claimant is not a company (see [BIM84050](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84050)).
4. The claim was made not later than the normal time limit, or, exceptionally, under the extended time limit for further claims (see [BIM84150](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84150)).
5. The claim covers two or five consecutive tax years (see [BIM84115](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84115)).
6. The profits dealt with by the claim are the trade profits after capital allowances and balancing charges (see [BIM84120](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84120)).
7. Of the profit figures, the lower figure is less than 75% of the higher figure (see [BIM84130](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim84130)).
