## Receipts taxable when they arise - but allow a properly computed provision for future maintenance

Where cemetery receipts include lump sums under contract to maintain in perpetuity the repair of the graves and monuments and/or the decoration of the graves, you should regard such sums as trading receipts.

However, you should allow a properly computed (see [BIM46500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim46500)) provision for the estimated future expenditure on the maintenance, repair and decoration of the graves and monuments (see London Cemetery Co Ltd v Barnes [1917] 7 TC 92).

For guidance on what this means in practice, see [BIM52510](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim52510).
