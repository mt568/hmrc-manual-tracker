title: 'Stock: non-trading transactions in stock: ways of disposing of or acquiring
  stock'
---

Stock can give rise to a profit or loss by:

* sale,
* appropriations to and from stock,
* insurance claim for part or total loss,
* barter transactions,
* use in the construction of a fixed asset,
* write off of all or part due to obsolescence or slow moving, see stock valuation ([BIM33100](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33100) onwards),
* fall in net realisable value, see stock valuation ([BIM33100](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33100) onwards),
* theft or other loss.

The tax value at which stock is disposed of, or acquired, depends on whether the disposal is in the course of the trade or otherwise.

Where the stock has been acquired, or is sold, in a trading transaction then [BIM33300](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33300) onwards discusses some more unusual transactions. Where the stock is acquired or disposed of as a result of the discontinuance of a business then [BIM33450](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33450) onwards gives the specific rules.
