title: 'Films and sound recordings: old regime for films: avoidance: individual exit
  schemes - who is affected by the legislation?'
---

S797(1), S800 Income Tax Act 2007

The legislation to counter individual exit schemes applies to individuals. There is separate legislation to counter corporate exits (see [BIM56560](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim56560)).

The legislation to prevent exits by individuals potentially applies to any individual who has benefited from film relief, i.e. someone who has:

* sustained a film related loss,
* in a trade carried on by the individual either alone or in partnership, and
* who has claimed relief against general income (sideways relief) or against chargeable gains for those losses (see [BIM85005](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim85005)).

A **film related loss** is any trading loss where any of the legislation at [BIM56010](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim56010) has been applied in computing the losses of the trade.

Such an individual will be subject to a stand-alone charge to Income Tax ([BIM56535](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim56535)) where:

* he has disposed of any of his rights to profits arising from the trade ([BIM56530](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim56530)), and
* an exit event has occurred (see [BIM56525](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim56525)).
