title: 'Financial traders - instruments and shares: avoidance cases'
---

Some avoidance schemes depend for their success, at least in part, in showing that a trade of financial dealing exists. Advice on the particular scheme involved should always be sought from Counter Avoidance, but in addressing the question of whether a trade is being carried on, you follow the same principles set out in this guidance.

Even if it is accepted that a trade is being carried on, there will be a question of whether a particular transaction of that trade is itself a trading transaction. In this situation, you need to apply the principles set out in [BIM20105](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim20105) and consider whether the transaction is a commercial one or one carried out for a fiscal purpose. Further guidance is available from from the Financial Products Team (CS&TD Business, Assets & International) in such cases.
