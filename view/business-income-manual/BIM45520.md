title: 'Specific deductions: insurance: employee indemnity'
---

Premiums on a policy paid by the employer to indemnify employees against legal action taken against them personally for actions in the course of the employment are allowable as a deduction in computing the employer’s trading profits. This is because the payments are not capital expenditure (see [BIM35000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim35000)) and they are incurred wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the trade (see [BIM37000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37000)).
