title: 'Deductions - scope of: starting point'
---

You cannot normally deduct expenditure in computing the profits of a business for Income Tax purposes if, either:

1. it falls within the scope of any of the statutory prohibitions (see [BIM42060](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42060)), or
2. it is not a proper debit item to be charged under generally accepted accounting practice against the incomings of the trade in computing the profits of that trade.

See:

Usher’s Wiltshire Brewery Ltd v Bruce 6TC399 [BIM37200](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37200)

Atherton v British Insulated & Helsby Cables Ltd 10TC155 [BIM35010](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim35010)

Morley v Lawford & Co 14TC229 [BIM37780](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37780)

The exception to this rule is where there is a statutory rule which permits a deduction, and that rule takes priority over any prohibitive rule which would otherwise apply. Permissive rules take priority over the exclusion of items not deductible in accordance with GAAP in all cases. See [BIM42070](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42070) for a list of permissive rules and [BIM42080](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42080) for the priority rules.
