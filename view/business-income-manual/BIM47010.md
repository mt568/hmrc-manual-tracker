title: 'Specific deductions: staffing costs: incentive awards'
---

A trader who has set up an incentive award scheme is allowed a deduction for providing awards under the scheme where:

* each award was provided under a firm contractual obligation, and
* was reasonable in relation to the task performed by the recipient and the value to the trader of having it performed, and
* was a genuine scheme undertaken by the trader for real business reasons.

If all the above tests are not met, the cost of the award may be disallowable as a gift or entertainment (see [BIM45000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim45000) onwards), or because it is not wholly and exclusively for a trade purpose (see [BIM37000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37000) onwards and [BIM42105](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim42105)).

For the Income Tax treatment of incentive awards in the hands of employees, see [EIM11200](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-income-manual/eim11200) onwards.

Guidance on deductions relating to share incentive schemes and share option schemes to provide employees with shares in their employing company or group is at [BIM44250](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim44250) onwards.
