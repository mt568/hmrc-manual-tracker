title: 'Solicitors: Interest received on behalf of non-resident clients'
---

S835C, S835E Income Tax Act 2007

A solicitor may receive interest on behalf of a person who is non-resident in the UK, either from a separate designated client account set up by the solicitor (see [BIM65805](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim65805)) or an account set up by the client, The solicitor should not be taxed on this savings income as their own except in the unlikely event that they act as the client’s ‘UK representative’ within the meaning of the above legislation. This would only be the case if the solicitor acts as the agent through whom the client carries on a trade in the UK. See [INTM264020](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/international-manual/intm264020).
