## Compensation falling to be dealt with as employment income

Although income arising from offices or employment is sometimes included in arriving at the measure of trading profits (see [EIM02500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-income-manual/eim02500)) such income is usually properly charged as employment income (see CIR v Brander & Cruickshank [1970] 46 TC 574).

It follows that, unless [BIM40140](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim40140) applies, compensation received in the course of such an office or employment or on its loss or termination should be considered in accordance with the employment income rules. See the instructions at [EIM00620](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-income-manual/eim00620) onwards and, for termination payments, at [EIM02500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/employment-income-manual/eim02500) onwards.
