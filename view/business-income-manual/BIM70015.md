### S31A ITTOIA 2005, S106C ITTOIA 2005

Cash basis receipts are any amounts received in connection with the trade during the basis period for the tax year and include:

* Cash and cheques
* Tips, fees and commissions
* Value of any payments ‘in kind’ for work done or goods sold
* Some capital receipts (see [BIM70020](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim70020))
* The value of trading stock on cessation of trade (see [BIM70025](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim70025))
* The value of work in progress on cessation of profession or vocation (see [BIM70025](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim70025))
* Just and reasonable additions in respect of non-commercial (non-arm's length) transactions (see below)
* Certain transitional adjustments on entering the cash basis (see [BIM70060](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim70060) onwards).

Any cash basis receipts which are received after the cessation of a trade using the cash basis are treated as post-cessation receipts (see [BIM90030](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim90030)).

### Amounts not reflecting commercial transactions

For businesses using the cash basis, there is no requirement to bring into account as a receipt the value of any goods taken from the business for personal use (see [BIM33630](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim33630)). Instead, a just and reasonable adjustment should be made to reflect the uncommercial transaction. So, for example, the cost of any such goods might be disallowed as an expense.
