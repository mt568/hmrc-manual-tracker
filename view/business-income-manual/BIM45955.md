title: 'Specific deductions: patents and royalties: expenditure on acquisition of
  know-how'
---

There is general guidance as to the meaning of know-how at [CA70010](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/capital-allowances-manual/ca70010). Broadly, ‘know-how’ consists of manufacturing techniques, technical knowledge and secret processes

For Corporation Tax purposes, know-how is dealt with under the intangible fixed assets regime (see [CIRD10000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird10000) onwards). Certain acquisitions of know-how by a company are excluded from the regime under its commencement rules where the acquisition is from a related party.

If the intangible asset regime does not apply, acquisition of know-how is generally dealt with, for Income Tax and Corporation Tax purposes, as part of the capital allowance system. For guidance see [CA70010](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/capital-allowances-manual/ca70010) onwards.

If there is a question as to whether the payments for know-how have a revenue character, this will need to be judged on its particular facts, in line with the principles explained at [BIM35000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim35000) onwards. You may also need to distinguish between payments for know-how, and franchise fees. Franchising is covered at [BIM57600](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim57600) onwards.

The guidance on receipts from know-how can be found at [CA72000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/capital-allowances-manual/ca72000) onwards.
