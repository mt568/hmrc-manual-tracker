## Intangible Assets

In relation to expenditure by companies in respect of intangible fixed assets see [CIRD12610](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-intangibles-research-and-development-manual/cird12610).

## Contact with Business Profits

Because of the nature of the legislation and the complexity of law relating to such criminal offences you should contact Business Profits at an early stage if you discover payments subject to the legislation.

A deduction is not allowable for certain crime-related payments in calculating the profits of a trade. This chapter describes the legislation and how it works. It is arranged as follows:
- [BIM43101 — Introduction](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43101)
- [BIM43110 — Accruals](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43110)
- [BIM43115 — Criminal payments: scope](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43115)
- [BIM43120 — Criminal payments: Terrorism Act](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43120)
- [BIM43125 — Criminal payments: Bribery Act](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43125)
- [BIM43126 — Bribery convictions](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43126)
- [BIM43130 — Criminal payments: handling of cases](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43130)
- [BIM43160 — Blackmail and extortion: overview](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43160)
- [BIM43165 — Blackmail and extortion: territorial limitations](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim43165)
