title: 'Private Finance Initiative (PFI): deemed premium on lease'
---

S218, S230, S232 Corporation Tax Act 2009

Where a private sector operator is granted a lease of less than 50 years by a public sector purchaser, as part of a PFI agreement, the terms of which impose obligations on the operator to carry out capital works on the premises, the lease may be deemed to have required the payment of a premium (see [PIM1212](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/property-income-manual/pim1212)).

In such circumstances the landlord is treated, for tax purposes, as receiving a revenue property business receipt based on a proportion of the deemed premium (see [PIM1205](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/property-income-manual/pim1205)).

Where the payer of the deemed premium sublets, without receiving a premium, the amount of the deemed premium treated as a revenue receipt of the landlord is treated as if it were a revenue expenseincurred by the payer. That is, it is deductible in computing the property income profit over the period of the sublease (see [PIM2310](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/property-income-manual/pim2310)). However, note that the amount of the expense is restricted where the carrying out of work gives rise to qualifying expenditure for capital allowances.

A similar provision applies where the payer of the deemed premium occupies the land for the purposes of their trade (see [BIM46255](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim46255)).

The relief is due even where the landlord is not chargeable on the deemed premium, e.g. because the landlord is exempt from tax (see [PIM2310](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/property-income-manual/pim2310)). However, in such cases it is necessary to ensure that an accurate valuation of the deemed premium is obtained (see [PIM1232](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/property-income-manual/pim1232)).
