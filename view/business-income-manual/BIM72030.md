title: 'Cash basis: expenses: overview'
---

The total amount of expenses of the trade to be brought into
account in calculating profits under the cash basis are those paid during the
tax year, subject to any adjustment required or authorised by law. So, a
business using the cash basis will not need to account for bad and doubtful
debts, for example.

Any payments made for both a business and a private purpose
should be apportioned, and only the business proportion should be included as
an allowable expense. For certain expenses, the allowable business proportion
can be calculated using simple flat rates, See [BIM75000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim75000) onwards
for more information.

Calculating profits or losses of a trade using the cash
basis does not usually affect the types of expenses which are allowable, with
the exception of the specific rules on

* Capital
  expenditure (see BIM72035)
* The
  restrictions for car hire (S48-S50B Income Tax (Trading and Other Income)
  Act 200) (see [BIM47714](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim47714))

Any expenses paid after the cessation of a trade using the
cash basis are treated as post-cessation expenses (see [BIM90080](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim90080)).
