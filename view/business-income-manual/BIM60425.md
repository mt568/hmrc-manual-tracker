title: 'Land transactions: Clearance applications: Refusal'
---

S770 Income Tax Act 2007, S831 Corporation Tax Act 2010

This guidance applies only if you are an officer authorised to deal with clearance applications (see [BIM60395](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60395)).You should not give a clearance where the conditions set out at [BIM60400](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60400) are not satisfied.

Sometimes the particulars provided by the applicant are obviously defective. The applicant may, for example, give particulars of a proposed sale but fail to say anything about the circumstances of the acquisition. For guidance on requesting further information to help you consider the application, see [BIM60415](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60415).

As discussed in [BIM60405](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60405), you should not refuse a clearance on the grounds that the transactions in land provisions apply without first consulting CTISA (Technical).

For a form of words that can be used to notify the taxpayer that clearance has been refused, see [BIM60430](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60430).

For guidance on accepting a clearance application, see [BIM60420](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60420).
