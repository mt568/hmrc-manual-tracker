title: 'Specific deductions: losses: foreign exchange gains and losses'

Guidance for accounting for foreign currency transactions is provided in FRS 102 Section 30 Foreign Currency Translation.

Related standards under other frameworks are:

FRS 105 Section 25 Foreign Currency Translation

IAS: IAS 21 The Effects of Changes in Foreign Currency

Old UK GAAP: SSAP 20 Foreign Currency Translation

In each case the respective standards contain specific rules on accounting for foreign exchange gains and losses.

### Companies

Exchange gains and losses that arise on the monetary assets or liabilities of companies are taxed or relieved under the loan relationship rules. See [CFM60000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/corporate-finance-manual/cfm60000) onwards.

### Unincorporated businesses

The tax treatment will often follow the accounting treatment. It will not follow the accounting treatment if the underlying asset is a capital asset of the business, unless the Marine Midland approach is followed (see [BIM39555](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim39555) and [BIM39560](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim39560)).

A full description of the treatment of foreign exchange gains and losses is in Statement of Practice SP 2/02 and [BIM39500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim39500) onwards.
