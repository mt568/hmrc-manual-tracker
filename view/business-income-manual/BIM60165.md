title: 'Measuring the profits (particular trades): land: trading transactions: reports
  to BAI Business Profits'
---

You should make a report to BAI Business Profits in any of the following circumstances:

* where the District Valuer's assistance is sought in a land valuation relating to a trade ([BIM51560](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim51560) onwards), agreement cannot be reached and a Tribunal appeal hearing may be necessary on the valuation point,
* in supervening trading cases which may proceed before the Tribunal ([BIM60060](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60060)),
* when you cannot reach agreement on the valuation of unsold stock in timeshare schemes ([BIM60155](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim60155)).
