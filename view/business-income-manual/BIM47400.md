The general principles determining whether a deduction is due in calculating the profits of a trade for subscriptions follow those for deductions generally. For general guidance on whether subscriptions and donations are paid wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the trade, see [BIM37500](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37500) to [BIM37510](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37510).

Associated guidance can be found as follows:

* mutual concerns - non trading - [BIM24010](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim24010)
* entertaining expenditure - [BIM45072](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim45072)
* charitable gifts and donations - [BIM45150](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim45150) onwards
* mutual insurance associations (note subscriptions solely for trade risks are allowable) -[BIM45590](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim45590)
* farmers - [BIM55280](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim55280)
* industrial and provident societies - [CTM40530](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm40530) to [CTM40535](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/company-taxation-manual/ctm40535).
