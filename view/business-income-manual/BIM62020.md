title: 'Measuring the profits (particular trades): Mineral extraction: miscellaneous
  receipts'
---

Miscellaneous receipts from the sale of waste products of a trade form part of the ordinary receipts of that trade, for example the sale of waste and slag by mining or iron working operators.

However, it will be a question of fact whether the sale of waste products, by the owner of the land on which the waste is deposited, amounts to a trade or an adventure in the nature of trade (see Shingler v P Williams & Sons [1933] 17TC574 and [BIM62055](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim62055)). If the sale does not amount to a trade or adventure in the nature of a trade, the profits may be chargeable under the miscellaneous income provisions; see [BIM100000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim100000) onwards.
