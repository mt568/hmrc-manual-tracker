title: 'Meaning of trade: exceptions and alternatives: contents'

This section deals with some activities which are at the fringes of trading income. If you need to make assessments you should consider whether alternative assessments are needed in order to protect the HMRC position. This procedure, which has been approved by the courts, is not considered to involve double taxation (see **Bye v Coren** 60 TC 116 and **Lord Advocate v McKenna** 61 TC 688).

It covers the following:
- [BIM22001 — Furnished lettings](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22001)
- [BIM22005 — Illegal activities - introduction](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22005)
- [BIM22007 — Illegal activities - the trading test](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22007)
- [BIM22008 — Illegal activities - no moral test](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22008)
- [BIM22010 — Illegal activities - what is a trade?](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22010)

- [BIM22016 — Betting and gambling - what is a bet?](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim22016)
