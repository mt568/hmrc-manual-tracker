title: 'Sub-postmasters: consideration for appointment as'
---

In the case of **Dhendsa v Richardson** [1997] SpC 134 the Special Commissioners decided that an introductory payment made by a sub-postmaster to the Post Office was capital expenditure and not wholly and exclusively for the purposes of the retail trade operated by the sub-postmaster from the same premises.

Such initial charges may be imposed in a variety of ways, for example as a lump sum or a reduction in the salary received in the first year.
