title: 'Specific deductions: rent and rates: introduction'
---

Expenses connected with premises or land occupied by a trader for the purposes of their trade will normally be allowed as a deduction unless the expenses relate to a capital matter (see [BIM35000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim35000) onwards).

Normal principles apply such as the ‘wholly and exclusively’ rule (see [BIM37000](https://www.gov.uk/hmrc-internal-manuals/business-income-manual/bim37000) onwards).

Examples of allowable expenses include:

* rent paid by a trader to the landlord for premises they use for business purposes
* business rates, Council Tax or ground rents
* the cost of insurance, whether of the building or of the contents
