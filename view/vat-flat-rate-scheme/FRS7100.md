title: 'Flat Rate Scheme Guidance: Trade Sectors: Reduction during the first year
  of VAT registration'
---

Under Regulation 55JB(1) VAT Regulations 1995, businesses that are in their first year of registration receive a 1% reduction to their flat rate. This reduction applies from the date a business joins the scheme, until the first anniversary of it becoming VAT registered.

It follows that if a business joins the scheme later than the date it registered for VAT, it will not be entitled to benefit from the reduction for a full year - or any reduction at all. [Notice 733](http://customs.hmrc.gov.uk/channelsPortalWebApp/channelsPortalWebApp.portal?_nfpb=true&_pageLabel=pageVAT_ShowContent&id=HMCE_CL_000345&propertyType=document#toc) gives an example of how this rule works in practice.

Under Regulation 55JB(2), the business is not entitled to any reduction if:

* the Commissioners received notification of, or otherwise became fully aware of, his liability to be registered more than one year after his EDR.

This means that where a business informs us, or we find that it should have been registered, more than one year after its EDR, it will not qualify for any reduction in flat rate.
